دراسة حاسوبية أولية للألفاظ النبوية في كتاب كنز العمال

A Preliminary Computational Study of the Language of the Hadith Reports in Kanz al-Ummāl

Author

Ali M. Al-Timimi, PhD

Published

July 9, 2026

1 Introduction

1.1 Background

Computational methods have become increasingly important in the study of historical texts, enabling large collections to be explored using statistical, linguistic, and machine learning approaches that would be impractical through manual inspection alone. While such methods have become commonplace for many modern languages, relatively few large-scale computational corpora exist for the classical hadith literature.

Kanz al-Ummāl fī Sunan al-Aqwāl wa al-Afʿāl is among the largest printed compilations of Prophetic traditions. Its size, sequential organization, and extensive source citations make it an attractive candidate for computational corpus construction. At the same time, its editorial structure presents numerous challenges for automatic parsing, including page continuations, abbreviated source citations, embedded section headings, publisher footnotes, and inconsistencies in the presentation of source material.

The present project seeks to develop a reproducible computational framework capable of transforming the printed work into a structured corpus suitable for quantitative analysis.

1.2 Objectives

The immediate objective of Version 1.0 is not to produce a definitive digital edition of Kanz al-Ummāl. Rather, the goal is to demonstrate the feasibility of constructing a machine-readable computational corpus that supports large-scale quantitative investigation of the Prophetic traditions contained within the collection.

Specific objectives include:

  • Construct a reproducible computational corpus from the digital source text.
  • Store the corpus within a DuckDB repository for efficient querying and downstream analysis.
  • Implement audit procedures to identify potential parsing problems.
  • Generate corpus-wide descriptive statistics.
  • Identify repeated linguistic expressions.
  • Analyze source abbreviations and narrator information.
  • Establish a foundation for future computational linguistic investigations.

1.3 Scope of Version 1.0

Version 1.0 should be regarded as a proof of concept. The project demonstrates that a large hadith collection can be transformed into a structured computational corpus and subjected to corpus-wide quantitative analysis using a reproducible processing pipeline.

The corpus is not yet considered complete. A number of parsing challenges remain, particularly regarding precise separation of the matn from source citations in a minority of reports. Consequently, the present release emphasizes conservative extraction, comprehensive auditing, and reproducibility over aggressive automation.

Despite these limitations, the current corpus already supports a broad range of descriptive and exploratory analyses, several of which are presented in the remainder of this report.

1.4 Organization of this Report

This report is organized into two major parts.

The first part describes the source materials used to construct the corpus, the computational framework developed for corpus construction, and the principal challenges encountered during automatic parsing.

The second part presents the analytical products generated from the current Version 1.0 corpus, including descriptive corpus statistics, repeated phrase discovery, source abbreviation analysis, and preliminary narrator extraction.

1.5 Development Philosophy

Throughout development, emphasis has been placed on reproducibility, transparency, and conservative corpus construction. Intermediate audit tables are retained, all analytical products are mirrored into DuckDB, and each stage of the workflow produces independently verifiable outputs.

Rather than attempting to maximize automatic parsing at the expense of reliability, the pipeline is designed to identify ambiguous cases explicitly and preserve them for later scholarly review. This philosophy ensures that future improvements to corpus fidelity can be incorporated incrementally without requiring reconstruction of the entire analytical framework.
